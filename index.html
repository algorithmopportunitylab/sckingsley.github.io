Hello, world!

I am a first year graduate student at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. My research focuses on the intersection of advanced (and emerging) technologies on economic growth, development, employment, and income distribution.

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a staff member for the late Senator Edward M. Kennedy in his labory policy office on the U.S. Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor and Pensions. After Senator Kennedy's passing, I joined the Administration of President Barack H. Obama as a presidentially appointed Congressional Liaison at the U.S. Department of Labor, where I had the honor to serve under Secretary of Labor Hilda L. Solis. Throughout my professional years in Washington, DC, I feel honored and grateful to have had the privilege to work on landmark pieces of legislation from pre-committee work to committee mark-up through final passage, including the Affordable Care Act (ACA) and the Matthew Shepard Hate Crimes Prevention Act of 2009.

In my spare time, I enjoy reading, traveling, cooking, soccer, outdoor activities, swimming, and learning foreign languages (Arabic, Farsi, Spanish, and a splash of German thus far!)
