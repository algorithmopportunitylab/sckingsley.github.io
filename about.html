Hello!

I am a researcher, public policy engineer and aspiring laywer. I was formerly a Associate Researcher for economics and technology policy at Microsoft corporation. I am currently a graduate student at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst.

My research interests and representative fields of work include:

  • computer science (AI, HCI, networking)
  • labor economics (industrial organization & online labor markets)
  • development (ict4d)
  • public policy (employment, education, privacy, civil + human rights)

Other adventures...

Prior to graduate school and working for Microsoft corporation, I was a public servant in Washington, D.C.

First, as a staff assistant for U.S. Senator Edward M. Kennedy (D-MA).

Later, as a Congressional Liaison for President Barack Hussein Obama at the U.S. Department of Labor in the Office of Congressional and Intergovernmental Affairs (OCIA).

Now, I am a human rights advocate who advocates for the rights of workers in technology, and advancing the representation and numbers of persons from marginalized communities in technology. I believe we are all born free and equal, and should be able to live our lives with equal dignity, autonomy, joy, and peace.
